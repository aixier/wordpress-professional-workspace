There's No Song of the Summer Because We're Having a Brainrot Summer™

Remember when we all knew the same songs? When "Song of the Summer" meant something? Yeah, that's dead. Welcome to Brainrot Summer™ – where everyone's watching different shows, listening to different music, and somehow we're all experiencing the same digital exhaustion. There's no Cruel Summer or Umbrella uniting us anymore. Instead, we have infinite TikTok sounds that live for 72 hours, memes that expire faster than milk, and algorithms so personalized that your FYP is basically your digital fingerprint. We're living through the death of monoculture in real-time. Netflix's biggest hits reach maybe 5% of viewers. Spotify has 100 million songs and zero consensus. Your internet is completely different from mine. The symptoms? Opening TikTok while watching Netflix. Speaking entirely in expired memes. Having the attention span of a goldfish on espresso. Knowing every micro-trend but remembering nothing. We consume content like breathing – constantly, unconsciously. Morning scroll, commute podcast, background YouTube, lunch TikTok spiral, evening Netflix-plus-phone combo, and "one more video" until 2 AM. Is this bad? Maybe. Is it fascinating? Absolutely. We're more connected yet more isolated in our content bubbles than ever. No shared cultural moments, just infinite personalized rabbit holes. So embrace your brainrot era. Accept the chaos. Curate your algorithm. And maybe, just maybe, touch some grass occasionally.
