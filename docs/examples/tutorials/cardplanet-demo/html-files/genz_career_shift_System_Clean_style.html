Why Gen Z Is Quitting $150K Tech Jobs for Coffee Shops
Post Content
Have you noticed your tech friends suddenly becoming baristas, nurses, or urban farmers? You're not imagining it. Gen Z is leading a mass exodus from Silicon Valley, trading six-figure salaries for work that actually makes them happy. The numbers are staggering: 73% of young tech workers report severe burnout, and 45% are actively planning career switches. What's driving this shift? It's not about being lazy or entitled. These digital natives discovered that debugging code at 2 AM while anxiety-scrolling through layoff news isn't the dream they were sold. They want tangible impact, human connection, and yes - the ability to actually disconnect. Sarah Chen left Google to start an urban farm. Marcus Johnson traded his Meta badge for a coffee shop apron. Riley Thompson went from scaling Amazon's servers to healing kids as a pediatric nurse. They all say the same thing: less money, more meaning. The most surprising part? 87% report being happier despite earning less. They're using their tech skills in new ways - project management for small businesses, data analysis for healthcare, systems thinking for agriculture. If you're feeling trapped in tech, you're not alone. Your analytical mind and problem-solving skills are valuable everywhere, not just in Silicon Valley.
